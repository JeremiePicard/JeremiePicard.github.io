Discrete event simulation

Discrete event simulation is a modeling approach widely used in decision support tools for logistics and supply chain management. It is the proper method for modeling complex environments, which have a lot of interactions between the modeled objects, where stochasticity is included in the system and where system operations are unstable and time dependent. Many projects were accomplished using simulation to resolve a specific question by my client. My second projet is featured in this video.

Statistical process control

Elaboration of single, double, multiple and progressive sampling plans by using opinion poll preparation, questionnaire to construct an analysis and interpretation of the results. The results can be interactively displayed thru dashboards and performance indicators. Experimentation, experience plans, taguchi methodology, kazen, six sigma and many statistical techniques can be used to statistically measure the quality of a process.
